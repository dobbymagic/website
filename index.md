## About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at University of Hohenheim, Stuttgart, Germany, in the Department of Eco-evolutionary modelling.

## Research Interests

My research aims to reveal fundamental relations between biotic interactions and evolutionary dynamics, with a particular focus on combining different types of interactions. For example, I have studied coevolutionary transitions between mutualism and antagonism and the evolutionary assembly of tripartite mutualistic-antagonistic ecological networks. The methods I use include adaptive dynamics, quantitative genetics, individual-based modelling and dynamical systems theory, employing both analytical and simulation-based approaches.
